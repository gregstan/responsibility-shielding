Responsibility Shielding OSF Preregistration - Greg Stanley

Overview
Research questions or hypotheses
Let B_CC, B_CH, and B_DIV denote the mean blameworthiness rating for Clark in the first vignette encountered (between-subjects), in the Choice–Choice, Choice–Chance, and Division conditions respectively.
Confirmatory Hypotheses
H1 (Shielding/No-shield contrast): Participants will rate Clark as more blameworthy in the Choice-Chance condition than in the Choice-Choice condition: B_CH not equal to B_CC
H2 (Division baseline vs shielded sequential): Participants will rate Clark as more blameworthy in the Division condition than in the Choice–Choice condition: B_DIV not equal to B_CC
I expect B_CH to be greater than B_CC and for B_DIV to be greater than B_CC, but the official hypothesis in each case uses a "not equal to" operator.
The Responsibility Shielding Effect, if found, is min(B_CH, B_DIV) - B_CC.
Exploratory: Compare CH vs DIV (direction not preregistered), and test whether effects replicate similarly across the two story scenarios (fireworks vs trolley).
Exploratory: The strength of the Responsibility Shielding Effect will be positively correlated with individualism, as measured by the INDCOL, negatively with reflectivity as measured by the CRT, and positively with the cognitive load manipulation.

Study type

Intention for causal interpretation

Blinding of experimental treatments
No blinding is involved.

Additional blinding during research or analysis
Participants are not informed of the study hypotheses or the existence/nature of alternative conditions. Condition assignment and stimulus presentation are automated by Qualtrics, and outcomes are self-report (no coder judgments).

Study design
Participants complete a web-based Qualtrics study. Design includes:

Between-subjects factors (randomized):

Story scenario: fireworks/pyrotechnic vs trolley

First vignette condition: Choice–Choice vs Choice–Chance vs Division

Cognitive load condition: high vs low (digit-span style manipulation; included as a secondary moderator)

Within-subject component:
Each participant reads all three main vignette structures (CC, CH, DIV) in counterbalanced order; however, the primary confirmatory analyses use only the first vignette encountered (between-subjects) to avoid carryover and consistency effects.

Primary outcome:
Clark blameworthiness rating (1–9 scale) after the vignette.

Secondary outcomes:
Punishment recommendation (years of prison) and forced-choice comparison items (2AFC) probing within-subject comparative judgments. Additional exploratory measures include CRT and INDCOL.

Comprehension checks and exclusions:
Participants failing comprehension checks are excluded from confirmatory analyses; incomplete surveys are excluded.

Sampling
Data collection procedures
Population / sampling frame: University of Michigan undergraduate subject pool participants recruited via SONA.

Recruitment method: Online study listing in SONA; participants complete a Qualtrics survey.

Eligibility: Age 18+; English fluency sufficient to read vignettes; no additional restrictions.

Procedure overview: Participants provide consent, receive instructions, complete the cognitive load manipulation (memorize a number), read and respond to multiple moral vignettes, complete forced‑choice comparisons and comprehension checks, then complete CRT, INDCOL, and demographics, and finally receive a completion code / credit confirmation.

Expected duration: ~30 minutes.

Data preparation: Data are exported from Qualtrics and preprocessed into an analysis‑ready dataset via a preregistered script (dropping Qualtrics metadata fields, harmonizing variable names, and constructing derived variables described below).

Sample size
Target N is determined by subject pool credit availability. The planned sample is approximately N = 240 completed participants (0.5 SONA credit each), although the sample may increase if Subject Pool allocates more subject hours, which often happens later in the school semester.

Randomization is expected to yield roughly equal cell sizes for the primary between‑subjects comparison across the three initial‑condition groups (approximately n ≈ 80 per initial condition), though small deviations due to attrition/exclusions are expected.

Sample size rationale
Primary sample size is constrained by the available subject pool credit (time/credit budget), not by a sequential stopping rule based on outcomes.

Sensitivity note: With n≈80 per group for a two‑group comparison, power is approximately 0.80 to detect standardized mean differences on the order of d≈0.45 with α=0.05 (two‑tailed). Interaction tests (e.g., story × condition) are expected to be less powered.

Starting and stopping rules
Pilot vs confirmatory boundary: Pilot data collected prior to this OSF registration were used only for refining materials and procedures and will not be included in confirmatory analyses. Confirmatory data collection begins only after the timestamp of this registration.

Stopping rule: Data collection ends when the study exhausts the subject‑pool credit allocation available for the semester (expected ~120 hours; may increase due to administrative reallocation) or at the end of the semester. No stopping decisions will be made based on observed outcomes.

Measured variables
Primary outcome: Clark blame rating after each vignette (9‑point Likert; 1 = not blameworthy at all, 9 = extremely blameworthy). Confirmatory analyses use Clark blame after the first main vignette only.

Analysis Plan
Statistical models
Primary confirmatory analysis (between‑subjects, blame DV):
Outcome: Clark blame rating after the first main vignette encountered.
Predictor: first‑encountered causal‑structure condition (3‑level factor: CC, CH, DIV).
Model: OLS regression (equivalently one‑way ANOVA), with planned contrasts.

Planned contrasts (confirmatory):

CH vs CC (expected CH > CC)

DIV vs CC (expected DIV > CC)

Covariate handling: Primary confirmatory tests will pool across story family and cognitive load, but we will also report models including story family and load as covariates (and optionally condition × story, condition × load interactions) as secondary analyses.

Secondary analyses (exploratory or clearly labeled secondary):
• Mixed‑effects model (random intercept for participant) predicting Clark blame across all three within‑subject conditions.
• 2AFC outcomes analyzed descriptively (proportions with 95% CIs) and, if desired, with logistic regression predicting the probability of choosing CH over CC (or DIV over CC).
• Proximate‑agent ratings: compare Bill blame in competent vs brain‑injury versions (diagnostic that the non‑agentic manipulation affects Bill‑blame as intended).
• Moderation: test whether cognitive load, CRT, or INDCOL predict the strength of deltas (e.g., CH−CC) or predict 2AFC directional choices.

Assumption checks: Given Likert outcomes, we will treat the 9‑point scale as approximately continuous for OLS; we will also report robustness checks using nonparametric tests (e.g., Mann‑Whitney) or ordinal regression where feasible.

Transformations
Likert outcomes will be analyzed as numeric 1–9 without transformation.

Punishment (years) is expected to be skewed; punishment analyses are exploratory and will be reported using both raw values and, if needed, a log(x+1) transformation or rank‑based tests as robustness checks.

Inference criteria
Alpha: 0.05 (two‑tailed) for the confirmatory family of tests on the primary DV.

Multiple comparisons: The two planned confirmatory contrasts (CH vs CC; DIV vs CC) will be treated as a small family and controlled using Holm‑Bonferroni correction across these two tests. All other analyses will be labeled as secondary or exploratory and interpreted accordingly.

Effect sizes: For pairwise contrasts we will report standardized mean differences (Cohen’s d) with confidence intervals; for omnibus effects we will report partial eta‑squared (or equivalent).

Data inclusion and exclusion
Inclusion: participants who consent, are age 18+, and complete the survey.

Primary exclusion: participants who fail any of the comprehension checks (must be 100% correct).

Additional exclusions: duplicate entries (same respondent), incomplete responses, or technical failures (e.g., missing primary DV).

Manipulation check handling: cognitive load recall accuracy will not be used as an exclusion criterion in confirmatory analyses; it will be reported as a manipulation check and used in secondary robustness analyses (e.g., rerunning key contrasts among those who recalled correctly).

Missing data
Qualtrics forces responses on key items; missing data is expected primarily from attrition (participants not finishing). Incomplete surveys will be excluded from confirmatory analyses. Otherwise analyses will be conducted on complete cases.

Other planned analysis
Compare patterns across story families (fireworks vs trolley) to evaluate generality (secondary).

Examine whether the within‑subject deltas (CH−CC; DIV−CC) are smaller than the between‑subjects first‑vignette effects, consistent with consistency pressures (exploratory).

Analyze 2AFC “equal vs different” endorsements as an indicator of how sharply participants distinguish conditions (secondary).

Exploratory analyses of punishment recommendations, including whether participants recommend incapacitation even when blame is low (e.g., brain‑injury proximate agent).

Responsibility Shielding Highlights:
• Blame concentrates on the last responsible actor, shielding whoever made the harm possible.
• Causally proximate actors only deflect blame if they can be attributed moral responsibility.
• The effect cannot be explained merely by diffusing responsibility across multiple actors.
• Shielding replicates across two story types and extends to punishment decisions.
• Those who set harm in motion may routinely hide behind those who carried it out.
